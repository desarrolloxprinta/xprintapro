FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is REGIUS a traditional consulting firm?

No. REGIUS is not limited to issuing recommendations or reports. It acts as a strategic operator, evaluating each situation and, when appropriate, structuring and coordinating real actions within a delimited framework.

How does REGIUS decide if it is involved in a case?

Every situation undergoes a structured preliminary evaluation. Context, viability, risks, regulatory framework and economic coherence are analyzed before defining if it is appropriate to move forward and under what type of participation.

Does REGIUS replace the Partner's relationship with the end customer?

No. The end customer belongs to the Strategic Partner. REGIUS does not compete for or absorb that relationship; it acts as a structural backup and platform for analysis and execution when complexity requires it.

Does REGIUS provide directly regulated services?

When an action requires activities subject to regulation (e.g., custody, financial services or escrow), these are coordinated with duly authorized providers or counterparties. REGIUS acts as a structurer and coordinator within its compliance framework.

In what types of situations does REGIUS intervene?

Mainly in corporate or financial contexts that require independent judgment, coordination between multiple actors and operational structuring. Each intervention is defined according to modality: strategic facilitation, structured intermediation or operational execution.

How is case confidentiality protected?

The action is carried out under strict confidentiality standards, including non-disclosure agreements where appropriate. Publicly documented cases are presented in a general and anonymized manner, without identifying elements.
